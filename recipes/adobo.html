Pinoy Adobo

Description

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish and cooking process in Philippine cuisine,
that involves meat marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns.
The meat is then browned in oil, and simmered in the marinade. This will make the meat juicy and tender.
The word adobo originated from the Spanish adobar meaning "marinade," "sauce" or "seasoning").
Adobo has occasionally been considered the unofficial national dish in the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • 1 kilo meat (chicken/pork)
  • 1/2 cup Vinegar
  • 1 1/3 cup Soy Sauce
  • 1 cup water (optional)
  • 2 medium-sized onions
  • 8 cloves garlic
  • 1 small pack of whole black peppercorns
  • 1 small pack of bay leaves
    • <1i>1/3 cup of oil

Steps

  1. In a tupperware, mix the vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves and peppercorns.
  2. Put the choice of meat inside tupperware and let it marinade for atleast 2 hours.
  3. After marinating, remove the chicken from the sauce.
  4. Heat up a pot then pour the cooking oil. When the oil is hot, brown the meat in the pot.
  5. When the chicken turns brown, saute the onions until they are soft and translucent.
  6. Pour the marinade into the pot slowly.
  7. Let it boil for 30-40 minutes until the sauce reduces and thickens.
  8. Optional: Add water as desired.
